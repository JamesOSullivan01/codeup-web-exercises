Forrest Gump FAQ

Q: When was the movie "Forrest Gump" released?
A: "Forrest Gump" was released on July 6, 1994.
Q: Who directed "Forrest Gump"?
A: The movie was directed by Robert Zemeckis.
Q: Who played the lead role of Forrest Gump?
A: Tom Hanks portrayed the character of Forrest Gump.
Q: What is the story of "Forrest Gump" about?
A: "Forrest Gump" follows the life journey of a simple-minded but kind-hearted man named Forrest Gump, who unintentionally becomes part of significant historical events in America.
Q: Did "Forrest Gump" win any Academy Awards?
A: Yes, "Forrest Gump" won six Academy Awards, including Best Picture, Best Director, and Best Actor for Tom Hanks.
Q: What are some notable quotes from the movie?
A: Memorable quotes from "Forrest Gump" include "Life is like a box of chocolates; you never know what you're gonna get" and "Stupid is as stupid does."
Q: Which historical events are depicted in the movie?
A: "Forrest Gump" features the portrayal of various historical events, such as the Vietnam War, the Civil Rights Movement, and the Watergate scandal.
Q: Who is Jenny in "Forrest Gump"?
A: Jenny Curran is the childhood friend and love interest of Forrest Gump, played by Robin Wright.
Q: What is the iconic running scene in the movie?
A: Forrest Gump embarks on a cross-country running spree, which becomes an iconic symbol throughout the film.
Q: What is the significance of the feather in the movie?
A: The feather serves as a recurring motif, representing the unpredictability and interconnectedness of life's events in "Forrest Gump."
